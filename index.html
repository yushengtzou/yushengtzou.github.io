On-Going Projects

Machine Learning for scRNA-seq Analysis in Tumors

In our project, we're harnessing the power of machine learning to dissect the complexity of scRNA-seq data generated from the tumor microenvironment. scRNA-seq offers us single-cell resolution, providing an in-depth view of cellular variability. The data's high dimensionality is a challenge, but this is where machine learning truly shines. It allows us to manage these complex data sets effectively, finding significant patterns within the data that are beyond human manual analysis capacity. The key role of machine learning is to turn this wealth of data into meaningful insights that can ultimately contribute to our understanding of tumor behavior.

We hope the research will contribute to a better understanding of the complex interactions within the tumor microenvironment and, ultimately, to the development of more effective cancer treatments.
